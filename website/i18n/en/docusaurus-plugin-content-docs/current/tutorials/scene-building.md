# Building a Scene

Now that you are familiar with the basics, let's build a complete scene. In this tutorial, we will create a "back-alley bookshop" step by step, chatting with the AI along the way.

## The Vision

A small, tucked-away bookshop at the end of a narrow alley. Walk along the cobblestone path and you will find a tiny shop surrounded by brick walls, with bookshelves packed behind a wooden counter. Warm, bare light bulbs softly illuminate the interior, and the sky outside the window glows with the colors of dusk -- that is the scene we are going for.

## Step 1: Create the Floor

We will start with the shop floor and the alley ground.

**You:**
> Create a flat floor, 10 meters by 10 meters. Make it look like aged cobblestone, with a muted gray-beige color

**AI action:** A thin box (Plane or thin Cube) is created with a cobblestone-style material applied.

**You:**
> Add a small step about 10 cm high along the edge of the floor, so you can tell where the inside of the shop ends and the outside begins

**AI action:** A low step-like object is added along one edge of the floor.

:::tip Check your work frequently
Take a screenshot here to see how things look.

**You:**
> Show me a screenshot
:::

## Step 2: Add the Walls

Since this is a bookshop, we need solid walls to enclose the interior.

**You:**
> Place brick walls along the north and east sides of the floor. 3 meters tall, reddish-brown brick with a slightly weathered look

**AI action:** Walls with a brick texture are created along the two edges.

**You:**
> On the south side, place a wall of the same height with a 1.5 meter wide wooden door in the center and a small glass window beside it

**AI action:** The south wall is placed with door and window frame objects integrated into it.

## Step 3: Place the Bookshelves

Time to add the main feature of the bookshop -- the bookshelves.

**You:**
> Line up 3 tall bookshelves along the north wall, reaching the ceiling. 5 shelves each, dark brown wood

**AI action:** Three tall bookshelf objects are placed along the wall, constructed from shelf panels and side panels.

**You:**
> Fill each shelf with tightly packed books -- colorful rectangular blocks with no gaps

**AI action:** Rectangular blocks of varying widths and colors (representing books) are arranged on each shelf.

**You:**
> Put 2 slightly shorter bookshelves against the east wall. 4 shelves on these ones

**AI action:** Two shorter bookshelves are added along the east wall.

:::info About the book representations
The books the AI creates are typically simple rectangular blocks. If you want more realistic books, you can use bookshelf assets from an asset store. For now, we are focusing on getting the shapes in place.
:::

## Step 4: Place the Wooden Counter

Let's create a space where customers can browse and chat with the shopkeeper.

**You:**
> Place a wooden counter slightly south of center in the room. 2 meters wide, 60 cm deep. Light-colored wood on top, dark brown on the sides

**AI action:** A counter object is created and placed at the specified location, with different materials on the top and sides.

**You:**
> Stack a few books on the counter, and add a small cash register too

**AI action:** Stacked books and a register-shaped object are placed on the counter.

Let's check the progress with a screenshot.

**You:**
> Take a screenshot from an angle that shows the whole scene

## Step 5: Set Up the Lighting

Lighting is key to setting the mood.

**You:**
> Hang 3 warm bare light bulbs from the ceiling, evenly spaced. Orange-tinted bulb color, with soft shadows

**AI action:** Three point lights are placed near the ceiling with a warm color temperature. Small sphere objects representing the bulbs are added as well.

**You:**
> Add a small desk lamp-style point light on the counter to illuminate the workspace

**AI action:** A small light is added near the counter, creating a local bright spot.

:::tip Lighting tips
A single light source tends to look flat. Combining a main light (the ceiling bulbs) with an accent light (the desk lamp) gives the scene more depth and atmosphere.
:::

## Step 6: Set the Skybox

The skybox provides the background sky for the scene.

**You:**
> Set the skybox to a dusk sky. A gradient of purple and orange, with the sun about to set

**AI action:** The scene background changes to a dusk sky. Combined with the light coming through the window, this gives the entire scene a cohesive feel.

**You:**
> Show me a screenshot

Check the overall result here.

## Using AI Feedback to Refine

If anything looks off in the screenshot, ask the AI to adjust it.

**You:**
> The counter seems too close to the bookshelves. Move it a bit further away

**AI action:** The counter's position is adjusted.

**You:**
> It is too dark overall. Turn up the brightness of the bare bulbs a bit

**AI action:** The light intensity is increased, brightening the scene.

**You:**
> Looks good. Show me a screenshot from the perspective of a customer standing in front of the counter

**AI action:** The camera moves to the height of a standing person in front of the counter, and a screenshot looking around the shop is taken.

:::info You can adjust as many times as you like
If something does not look right, feel free to ask for adjustments as many times as you need. The whole point of OpenForge MCP is this iterative back-and-forth with the AI to refine your scene.
:::

## Complete

Congratulations -- the back-alley bookshop scene is done. Here is a recap of what we built:

1. **Cobblestone floor** -- Created the foundation of the scene
2. **Brick walls** -- Enclosed the space
3. **Bookshelves** -- Placed the centerpiece of the bookshop
4. **Wooden counter** -- Created the shop area
5. **Warm bare bulbs** -- Set the atmosphere
6. **Dusk skybox** -- Established the background

Every step was done entirely through conversation with the AI. In the next tutorial, you will learn how to create an original model using Blender.

:::warning Remember to save
Make sure to save the scene you just built. Just tell the AI "Save" and it will take care of it. See the [Save and Restore](./save-restore.md) tutorial for details.
:::
